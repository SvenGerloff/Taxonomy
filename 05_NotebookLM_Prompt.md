Module Theme: The European Union Taxonomy: A Framework for Sustainability

2. The Crisis/Challenge: The Six Objectives
The facility's current operations rely heavily on traditional, linear models. The challenge is presented by an auditor: the farm must demonstrate substantial contribution to at least one of the EU Taxonomy's six environmental objectives, while doing "no significant harm" to the others.

3. The Learning Phase: Practical Application & Decision Points

- Zone B: The Hatchery (Sustainable Use and Protection of Water and Marine Resources)
  Decision: Implement a Recirculating Aquaculture System (RAS), treating and reusing 95% of the water.
- Zone C: Waste Processing (Transition to a Circular Economy & Pollution Prevention)
  Decision: Capture sludge and process it into biogas and agricultural fertilizer, ensuring zero nutrient runoff.
- Zone D: The Coastal Seabed (Protection and Restoration of Biodiversity and Ecosystems)
  Decision: Deploy benthic monitoring sensors and implement a fallowing system to let native Posidonia oceanica (seagrass) meadows regenerate.

4. Mastery Demonstration: The Taxonomy Action Plan
The climax of the scenario requires the learner to synthesize these individual decisions into a cohesive "Taxonomy Action Plan" presented to the investors, linking specific operational upgrades to the correct EU Taxonomy objectives.

5. Impact Visualization Panel
Interactive dashboard showing:

- Economic: Green financing secured, operational energy costs reduced.
- Social: Creation of specialized green jobs, enhanced community trust.
