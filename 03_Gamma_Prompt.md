# EU Taxonomy for Aquaculture
- A practical rulebook for proving an investment is environmentally sustainable
- Used by banks, grant programs, and investors
- Not about slogans
- About evidence
[Image suggestion: Aquaculture manager reviewing farm data]

---

# What it is
- EU Taxonomy = one shared EU classification system
- It checks if a farm activity is genuinely sustainable
- It reduces “greenwashing”
- It links real farm upgrades to finance, permits, and reporting

---

# Why it matters on a farm
- Even if your farm is not forced to report, funders still look at Taxonomy logic
- It can support access to:
- EMFAF grants
- LIFE funding
- Horizon Europe
- EIB green loans

---

# The 6 objectives
- 1. Climate mitigation
- 2. Climate adaptation
- 3. Water and marine resources
- 4. Circular economy
- 5. Pollution prevention
- 6. Biodiversity and ecosystems

---

# Example 1: diesel to hybrid power
- Replace diesel generators with solar-wind hybrid power
- This supports climate mitigation
- Add stronger moorings and temperature sensors
- That supports climate adaptation
- Same farm, different Taxonomy objectives

---

# Example 2: RAS hatchery upgrade
- A Recirculating Aquaculture System reuses 95%+ of hatchery water
- This supports water and marine resources
- If discharge is controlled and measured, it also helps pollution prevention
[Image suggestion: Hatchery water loop diagram]

---

# Example 3: sludge as a resource
- Capture sludge and process it into biogas or fertilizer
- This supports circular economy
- If runoff is prevented and chemicals are controlled, it also supports pollution prevention
- Waste becomes documented output

---

# The 4 main rules
- 1. Help at least one objective in a substantial way
- 2. Do No Significant Harm to the other objectives
- 3. Meet minimum social safeguards
- 4. Meet technical screening criteria where required

---

# How to prove it
- Use objective records, not claims
- Fuel logs
- kWh data
- Water reuse %
- Discharge tests
- Waste transfer notes
- Benthic surveys
- Chemical inventory
- Medicine records
- Build a simple evidence matrix

---
